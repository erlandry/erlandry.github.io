Sol LeWitt "Wall Drawing #38"

  One might think Sol LeWitt's work would bypass a conservator almost completely, but a 25 year long exhibition at Mass MOCA of Sol LeWitt's wall drawings features a work that required some intervention. Wall Drawing #38 is a work made using tissue paper and a peg board, where the directions are "....". The piece needed to be reinstalled every two years because the yellow tissue paper would fade almost to white. John Hogan asked the conservation lab to extend the lightfastness of the tissue paper. I performed all steps of this projects attempting to match the both the color and texture of the original paper to the best of my ability. In order to do this I:

Tested different pigments and binders to match the color and texture of the orignal paper.

Tried different applications on the paper and drying techniques.

After coming up with a satisfactory outcome, built a mockup using the real and fabricated tissue paper.

I took color measurements of the original tissue paper and painted paper using iControl software which measured the colors white value, red/green axis value and yellow/blue axis value.

I performed a fading test on the paper. They were placed in an exposure cabinet at 200,000 lux for 24 hours.

I took measurements of the paper after their time in an exposure cabinet. The original paper faded as expected (the yellow almost entirely). The yellow paper I had painted had only faded 0.35 delta E.

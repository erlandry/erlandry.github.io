Pictor Classicus Sum:
Giorgio de Chirico and the Avant-Garde

     In the well-known essay “Avant-Garde and Kitsch”, the art critic Clement Greenberg erroneously condemns the work of Giorgio de Chirico, the founder of Metaphysical Painting as kitsch. In a few words, Greenberg accuses De Chirico’s late work of being a watered down version of genuine culture that aids fascist regimes. This paper explains Greenberg’s oversight by demonstrating how De Chirico’s late paintings are progressive and fit Greenberg’s definition of avant-garde.
